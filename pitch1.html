AI Project Case: Window Installation Control

Building MVP for a private building restoration company in European Country.

Initial task: to perform control over window installation works in the process of buildings reconstruction.
As the result of the project the following 2 models were trained:

1. Object Detection

01

- Two CNNs used, common: YOLOv5, own: AutoML
- Dataset: more than 10000 photos
- Detection accuracy: 97%
- Detection speed: 500 ms on CPU, 15 ms on GPU

2. Segmentation

02

- One CNN used: own architecture
- Dataset: more than 10000 photos
- Segmentation accuracy: over 95% (per pixel)
- Segmentation speed: 45 ms on GPU

AI Optimizer

Prototype for efficient use of hardware resources for HPC jobs. On-demand ml training and inference execution.

  • Type
  • HPC Marketplace
  • Market
  • World
  • Industry
  • Datacenters, Mining Facilities
  • Timeline
  • 1 Year
  • Man-hour
  • 1000
  • Key Features
    • Execution of training and inference jobs, integration into existing solutions (i.e. Lenovo, Nvidia, Core scientific)

A marketplace connecting providers of high performance computing (hpc) hardware and potentials customers with hpc jobs. Owners of high performance hardware suffer from unsufficient resource allocation. At the same time long-term contracts or even usage of cloud-based training machines can be very expensive. The software deployed on the machines uses the low-usage phases for cryptocurrency mining. Researches and data-scientists could submit jobs as docker containers. The software would distribute the jobs to the most fitting datacenter, based on required capabilities and available capacity (i.e. video rendering demands different capabilities then machine learning model training). Consumers save money by paying for the GPU time only. Vendors make money by utilizing idle times.

TECHNICAL SOLUTION

01

Development of a data model for cryptocurrency earnings prediction for different hardware types and market scenarios.

02

Creation of distribution and execution environment for AI jobs. Creation of own server-controller deployed to target system.

03

Building of market place for job submission and price determination.

04

Integration of the system and deployment to test datacenters.

RedVault CRE

Understanding and Analysis of Contracts and Operational Expenses for Commercial Real Estate

  • Type
  • Multi-tenancy platform
  • Market
  • Canada, US
  • Industry
  • Commercial Real Estate
  • Timeline
  • 2 years
  • Man-hour
  • 5000
  • Key Features
    • Understanding of natural and legal language, creation of omnibus contracts, digitalization of contracts.

Companies with a large number of leases (i.e. banks) face several problems in the management of those leases. Two of the largest problems are:
- Control of operational expense statements and evaluation of costs per GLA in a building.
- Understanding of the current state of a lease contract through a history of changes and ammendments.

The solution is capable of:
- Recognition of relevant parts of a document (image recognition, image processing). Understanding and digitazing operational expense statements. Categorizing single positions in a extendable and self-learning ontology.
- Evaluating expenses based on building/lease types, geo-locations, years etc.
- Digitalizing and recreating a structure of contracts.
- Creating a contract ammendment timeline. Creating an omnibus contract.
- Understanding a contract and answering natural language questions about a contract (i.e. who has the right to audit?)
- Tagging and highlighting different topics within a contract for auditor attention.

TECHNICAL SOLUTION

01

Development of an image processing pipeline to create structured digitalized documents from scanned documents.

02

Creation and training of an AI model for undestanding of operational expense statements.

03

Creation of a pipeline and training of several models for processing of contracts.

04

Development of a multi-tenant portal for customers (tenants) and auditors for collaborative work on the contracts.
